\chapter{Initial Feasibility Study}

\section{Introduction}

This chapter presents an initial feasibility study for the major problems identified in Chapter 1 of the Information System Analysis report on NextStepBD. The purpose of this chapter is to evaluate whether the proposed improvements are technically possible, economically reasonable, and operationally acceptable for the organization.

The feasibility analysis follows the standard Technical, Economic, and Operational (TEO) framework. Each problem is examined from three perspectives:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Technical Feasibility:} Whether the proposed solution can be implemented using available technology, tools, and technical skills.
    \item \textbf{Economic Feasibility:} Whether the expected benefits justify the cost of implementation, training, and maintenance.
    \item \textbf{Operational Feasibility:} Whether the organization can realistically adopt the solution within its existing workflow, culture, and manpower.
\end{itemize}

NextStepBD is a software development, automation, and consultancy-based company. The company currently utilizes various digital tools including WhatsApp, Slack, Email, GitHub, Microsoft Excel, and Google Sheets for communication, development, reporting, and coordination purposes. Although these tools adequately support daily operations, the absence of a centralized and integrated information system creates significant challenges including operational inefficiency, data duplication, tracking difficulty, and substantial dependency on manual coordination mechanisms.

The goal of this feasibility study is to identify which improvement initiatives should be prioritized to enable NextStepBD to develop a more structured, scalable, and efficient operational system capable of supporting sustained organizational growth.

\section{Initial Feasibility Analysis}

\subsection{Problem 01: Fragmented System and Lack of Integration}

\subsubsection{Statement of the Problem}

NextStepBD currently operates multiple disconnected platforms including WhatsApp, GitHub, Google Sheets, Excel, Slack, and Email. While each tool performs a useful function, there is no centralized system that effectively connects communication, task management, development progress, client requirements, and reporting functions.

Consequently, information is scattered across different platforms. Employees may need to manually transfer data between systems, which increases the probability of errors, data duplication, and operational delays. Furthermore, managers face considerable difficulty in obtaining a unified and accurate view of project status across the organization.

\subsubsection{Summary of Findings}

Introducing an integrated workflow management system is technically feasible given that numerous cloud-based platforms already support seamless integration with GitHub, Google Sheets, Slack, Email, and other essential business tools. The solution is economically feasible due to its potential for reducing manual effort, rework, and coordination delays. Operationally, the solution is feasible if implemented gradually, commencing with one or two pilot projects before expanding across the entire organization.

\subsubsection{Details of Findings}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Technical Feasibility:} Platforms such as Jira, ClickUp, Notion, Trello, Zoho Projects, and Monday.com can integrate task tracking, document storage, reporting, and communication functionalities. These platforms can also establish connections with GitHub and Google Workspace. Consequently, the company does not need to develop the entire system from the beginning.
    
    \item \textbf{Economic Feasibility:} The estimated annual cost of a cloud-based integrated system for a small to medium-sized team may range from BDT 3 to 8 lakhs, depending on the number of users and selected platform. This cost is justified because the system can substantially reduce manual coordination efforts, reporting time, and project delays.
    
    \item \textbf{Operational Feasibility:} Since NextStepBD already utilizes digital tools, employees are familiar with online platforms. However, comprehensive training and clear usage policies will be required. The system should be introduced gradually to prevent employees from experiencing sudden pressure.
    
    \item \textbf{Risk Assessment:} The primary risk involves resistance from employees who are accustomed to existing informal tools. This risk can be mitigated through structured training programs, management support, and a phased implementation plan.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendation}

NextStepBD should adopt a centralized project and workflow management platform within a period of 3 to 4 months. The selected system should effectively integrate task assignment, GitHub development updates, client requirements tracking, reporting capabilities, and internal communication channels. A pilot implementation should be conducted with selected projects prior to full organizational deployment.

\subsection{Problem 02: Over-Reliance on Informal Communication Channels}

\subsubsection{Statement of the Problem}

WhatsApp is heavily utilized by NextStepBD for both client communication and internal coordination purposes. While WhatsApp provides convenient fast communication, it is not designed for structured project management, official documentation, task assignment, or decision tracking functionalities.

Important messages, client requirements, and project decisions may become buried within long chat threads. This creates significant difficulty in searching past discussions, identifying responsible persons, and maintaining appropriate accountability records.

\subsubsection{Summary of Findings}

A structured communication and documentation policy is feasible for NextStepBD. The company does not need to discontinue using WhatsApp completely; instead, WhatsApp can be retained for quick communication while formal decisions, requirements, task assignments, and approvals should be recorded in the centralized project system.

\subsubsection{Details of Findings}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Technical Feasibility:} Platforms such as Slack, Microsoft Teams, Google Chat, and project management tools can organize communication into channels, threads, project spaces, and searchable records. These tools can also integrate with task boards and GitHub repositories.
    
    \item \textbf{Economic Feasibility:} The company can commence with free or low-cost subscription plans. Since Slack is already utilized for international collaboration, enhanced utilization of Slack or a similar tool would not require a substantial additional investment.
    
    \item \textbf{Operational Feasibility:} Employees are already comfortable with chat-based communication methodologies. Therefore, transitioning important project communication from informal WhatsApp groups to structured project channels is a realistic objective. However, management must clearly define which type of communication belongs to which platform.
    
    \item \textbf{Risk Assessment:} Employees may continue using WhatsApp out of habit. This behavior can be controlled by establishing clear policies: urgent messages may be communicated via WhatsApp, but all requirements, approvals, and task updates must be documented in the official system.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendation}

NextStepBD should introduce a formal communication policy. WhatsApp should remain available for quick notifications and emergency communication, but official client requirements, decisions, approvals, and task updates should be recorded within the project management system or designated Slack channels.

\subsection{Problem 03: Unstructured Workflow and Weak Requirement Management}

\subsubsection{Statement of the Problem}

Client requirements at NextStepBD are frequently received through WhatsApp messages, shared documents, or informal discussions. Due to the absence of a standardized requirement collection format, requirements may be incomplete, unclear, or subject to frequent changes.

This situation creates considerable confusion among developers and managers. Developers may need to make assumptions regarding missing details, which substantially increases the risk of rework, project delay, and potential mismatch between client expectations and final delivery outcomes.

\subsubsection{Summary of Findings}

A structured requirement management process is highly feasible for NextStepBD. The company can introduce standard requirement templates, client approval checkpoints, and formal change request forms. These improvements do not require substantial infrastructure investment but can significantly improve project clarity and delivery quality.

\subsubsection{Details of Findings}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Technical Feasibility:} Requirement management can be effectively implemented using Google Forms, Notion, Jira, Trello, ClickUp, or a custom internal portal. These tools can store requirement descriptions, attachments, priority levels, acceptance criteria, and approval status information.
    
    \item \textbf{Economic Feasibility:} The associated cost is minimal because the company already utilizes Google Workspace and other digital tools. The primary investment will involve time spent designing templates and training employees on new procedures.
    
    \item \textbf{Operational Feasibility:} Both managers and developers will benefit directly from clearer requirements. Clients may also appreciate a structured process because it reduces misunderstanding and improves project outcomes. However, the process should remain simple to prevent clients from feeling overloaded.
    
    \item \textbf{Risk Assessment:} If the requirement form is excessively complicated, clients and managers may avoid utilizing it. Therefore, the format should be concise and practical in design.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendation}

NextStepBD should create a standard Software Requirement Specification (SRS) template or lightweight requirement form. Every new project or feature request should include the following elements: objective, user role, functional requirement, non-functional requirement, priority level, deadline, and approval status. All requirement changes should be handled through a formal change request process.

\subsection{Problem 04: Absence of Centralized Task and Project Management System}

\subsubsection{Statement of the Problem}

Task assignment and project progress tracking at NextStepBD are currently managed primarily through manual communication channels. Managers assign tasks through WhatsApp or direct messages, while developers utilize GitHub primarily for code version control purposes. There is no single dashboard available for tracking deadlines, dependencies, progress updates, bottlenecks, and individual responsibilities.

This situation presents considerable challenges for managers in monitoring all tasks efficiently. Developers may also lack clarity regarding priority levels, deadlines, and task dependencies.

\subsubsection{Summary of Findings}

A centralized task and project management system is both technically and economically feasible for NextStepBD. Since the company already utilizes GitHub, a project management system can be effectively integrated with GitHub issues, pull requests, and development workflows. This integration will substantially improve visibility, accountability, and delivery planning capabilities.

\subsubsection{Details of Findings}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Technical Feasibility:} Platforms such as GitHub Projects, Jira, ClickUp, Trello, and Zoho Projects can provide task boards, Kanban views, sprint planning capabilities, deadline tracking, and assignment management. GitHub Projects may be particularly suitable because the development team already utilizes GitHub extensively.
    
    \item \textbf{Economic Feasibility:} GitHub Projects can be utilized with minimal additional cost. More advanced platforms may require subscription fees, but the benefit of improved project control can justify the associated expenditure.
    
    \item \textbf{Operational Feasibility:} Developers are already familiar with GitHub, so adopting GitHub Issues or GitHub Projects would be easier than introducing a completely new system. Managers will require training on dashboard monitoring and task reporting procedures.
    
    \item \textbf{Risk Assessment:} If tasks are not updated regularly, the dashboard will become unreliable. Therefore, task updates should be integrated as part of the daily workflow routine.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendation}

NextStepBD should implement a centralized task management system utilizing GitHub Projects or a similar project management tool. Each task should include a responsible person, deadline, priority level, status, and related GitHub issue or pull request reference. Managers should review the dashboard regularly to identify delays and potential bottlenecks.

\subsection{Problem 05: Inefficient Data Management and Reporting}

\subsubsection{Statement of the Problem}

NextStepBD relies heavily on Microsoft Excel and Google Sheets for reporting purposes, financial data handling, client-side collaboration, and operational records. Although these tools are useful for basic data handling, they become increasingly inefficient when data volume increases or when multiple people update files manually.

Data may become scattered across different sheets, resulting in inconsistency, duplication, and significant difficulty in generating real-time reports.

\subsubsection{Summary of Findings}

Improving the reporting system is feasible through implementation of a centralized database and dashboard-based reporting approach. The company can commence with a simple system that collects project, client, sales, and operational data in a unified location and generates automated reports.

\subsubsection{Details of Findings}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Technical Feasibility:} Tools such as Google Looker Studio, Microsoft Power BI, Airtable, Zoho Analytics, or a custom web-based dashboard can be effectively utilized. These tools can establish connections with spreadsheets, databases, and project management systems.
    
    \item \textbf{Economic Feasibility:} The initial cost can be controlled by utilizing existing Google Sheets with integrated dashboard tools. A fully custom reporting system may incur higher costs, but it can be developed gradually by the internal team.
    
    \item \textbf{Operational Feasibility:} Managers and administrative staff already utilize spreadsheets, so dashboard-based reporting would be easier to adopt. The primary change will involve maintaining clean and standardized data practices.
    
    \item \textbf{Risk Assessment:} Poor data entry discipline can reduce report accuracy. The company should establish standard data formats, access control policies, and validation rules.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendation}

NextStepBD should introduce a centralized reporting dashboard. In the first phase, Google Sheets can remain as the primary data source, but reports should be automated using dashboard tools. In the long term, the company should migrate important operational data to a centralized database system.

\subsection{Problem 06: Scalability and Dependency Issues}

\subsubsection{Statement of the Problem}

As NextStepBD continues to grow, its current workflow creates substantial dependency on specific individuals, particularly managers who act as intermediaries between clients and developers. If these key individuals are unavailable, task assignment, clarification, reporting, and decision-making processes may be significantly delayed.

The absence of standardized processes also makes it difficult to onboard new employees effectively and manage larger teams in an efficient manner.

\subsubsection{Summary of Findings}

Scalability can be substantially improved by standardizing workflow procedures, documenting processes comprehensively, and introducing role-based access and responsibility frameworks. This approach is technically feasible because process documentation and workflow automation tools are readily available in the market. It is economically justified because it reduces dependency risk and improves business continuity.

\subsubsection{Details of Findings}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Technical Feasibility:} Standard operating procedures, onboarding checklists, knowledge bases, and workflow automation can be effectively implemented using Notion, Confluence, Google Drive, GitHub Wiki, or a custom internal portal.
    
    \item \textbf{Economic Feasibility:} The associated cost is moderate because the primary investment involves process design, documentation, and training efforts. The benefits include faster onboarding processes, fewer operational delays, and reduced dependency on individual managers.
    
    \item \textbf{Operational Feasibility:} The flat organizational structure of NextStepBD supports rapid decision-making, but organizational growth necessitates more formal role definitions. Employees may accept this change if it reduces confusion and improves work clarity.
    
    \item \textbf{Risk Assessment:} Documentation may become outdated if not maintained regularly. A responsible person or team should be designated to review process documents at established intervals.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendation}

NextStepBD should create a centralized knowledge base and standard operating procedures covering project intake, requirement analysis, task assignment, reporting, client communication, and employee onboarding processes. The company should also establish backup role structures to ensure that work does not become dependent on any single individual.

\section{Conclusion}

The initial feasibility study demonstrates that the problems identified in NextStepBD's current operational system are solvable through practical and cost-effective information system improvements. The company does not need to replace all existing tools immediately. Instead, it should connect and organize them through a centralized workflow management structure, integrated task management capabilities, formalized requirement management processes, structured communication channels, and automated reporting mechanisms.

The highest priority should be assigned to centralized task management implementation, structured requirement collection processes, and tool integration initiatives because these directly reduce operational confusion, manual coordination requirements, and project delays. Communication policy establishment and reporting automation should be implemented in the subsequent phase, followed by process documentation and scalability improvements.

Overall, the proposed improvements are technically feasible, economically justified, and operationally realistic. If implemented in a phased manner, the improved system will help NextStepBD achieve better coordination, stronger accountability standards, faster reporting capabilities, improved client satisfaction levels, and sustainable organizational growth.
